  \textit{\color{truyengold}``Ban gieo gi, ban gat nay.''}\\[4pt]
  \textit{(As you sow, so shall you reap.)}\\[4pt]

\section{Nguoi Nong Dan Cuu Fleming Cuu Churchill}
\begin{truyen}{Nguoi Nong Dan Cuu Fleming Cuu Churchill}{Truyen Thuc Te --- Scotland 1886}
\chuhoa{M}{}M ot ngay o Scotland, mot cau be quy ngheo bi sa xuong dam lay va keu cuu tuyet vong.\\
\textit{(One day in Scotland, a poor farm boy fell into a boggy swamp and cried out desperately for help.)}

Mot nong dan nghe tieng keu, bo viec lai cuu cau be len --- khong nghi ngoi, khong doi biet on.\\
\textit{(A farmer heard the cries, dropped his work and pulled the boy out --- without hesitation, without expecting thanks.)}

Hom sau, mot quy ong do xe ngua den nha nguoi nong dan nay, noi: 'Con trai toi muon cam on ong.'\\
\textit{(The next day, a well-dressed gentleman drove a carriage to the farmer's home, saying: 'My son wants to thank you.')}

Ong quy ong de nghi tra on bang cach cho con trai nguoi nong dan di hoc y khoa tai Luong Y --- nguoi nong dan khiem ton nhan loi.\\
\textit{(The gentleman offered to repay by sending the farmer's son to study medicine in London --- the humble farmer accepted.)}

Con trai nguoi nong dan tro thanh Alexander Fleming, nguoi phat minh ra Penicillin; va cau be duoc cuu nam xua chinh la Winston Churchill.\\
\textit{(The farmer's son became Alexander Fleming, who discovered Penicillin; and the boy saved long ago was Winston Churchill himself.)}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Mot hanh dong tot tu nguyen den roi co the cuu song ca the gioi --- vi chung ta khong biet dieu gi se xay ra tu long nhan ai.\\
  \textit{(One spontaneous act of kindness can save the whole world --- for we never know what ripples flow from simple human compassion.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Nguoi Trong Cay Bong Mat Cho Doi Sau}
\begin{truyen}{Nguoi Trong Cay Bong Mat Cho Doi Sau}{Triet Ly Co --- Trung Dong co dai}
\chuhoa{M}{}M ot cu ong gia bo co the dang trong cay cho nho.\\
\textit{(An old man was seen planting date palm saplings.)}

Nguoi di duong hoi: 'Ong tuoi dau roi ma trong cay cho nho --- ong co the song de an khong?'\\
\textit{(A passerby asked: 'You are so old and planting date palms --- will you live long enough to eat them?')}

Cu ong tra loi: 'Khi toi sinh ra, da co cho san khi toi an --- nguoi xua trong ma khong biet toi la ai.'\\
\textit{(The old man replied: 'When I was born, there were already dates waiting for me --- someone had planted without knowing me.')}

'Toi trong hom nay de nhung nguoi sinh ra sau toi se co cho an --- du ho khong biet toi la ai.'\\
\textit{('I plant today so those born after me will have dates to eat --- though they will never know my name.')}

Nguoi di duong ngoi xuong, im lang suy nghi --- roi cung cam lay mot cay con trong xuong dat.\\
\textit{(The passerby sat down, fell silent in thought --- then also picked up a small sapling and planted it in the earth.)}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Hanh dong dep nhat khong phai la hanh dong de nguoi ta nho on ban --- ma la hanh dong ban lam cho nguoi ban khong bao gio gap.\\
  \textit{(The most beautiful action is not one done to be remembered --- it is the one you do for someone you will never meet.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Andrew Carnegie Va Nguon Goc Cua Long Tu Thien}
\begin{truyen}{Andrew Carnegie Va Nguon Goc Cua Long Tu Thien}{Andrew Carnegie --- Hoa Ky 1900}
\chuhoa{A}{}A ndrew Carnegie bat dau cuoc doi tu mot co be lam viec trong nha may khi moi 13 tuoi, luong mot do la mot tuan.\\
\textit{(Andrew Carnegie began life as a 13-year-old factory worker earning one dollar a week.)}

Mot ong chu nha may tung cho cau be nghe nho thu vien cua ong --- dieu do thay doi cuoc doi Carnegie.\\
\textit{(A factory owner once allowed the young boy to use his private library --- that changed Carnegie's life.)}

Khi tro nen giau co, Carnegie nho lai dieu do: 'Ong ay cho toi dieu gi? Kien thuc. Kien thuc lam thay doi tat ca.'\\
\textit{(When Carnegie became wealthy, he remembered: 'What did he give me? Knowledge. Knowledge changes everything.')}

Carnegie sau do xay dung 2.509 thu vien cong cong tren khap nuoc My --- tu tien cua chinh minh.\\
\textit{(Carnegie then built 2,509 public libraries across America --- from his own funds.)}

Ong noi: 'Nguoi chet giau la nguoi chet nho.' Ong tang het tai san truoc khi mat --- va ten ong song mai.\\
\textit{(He said: 'The man who dies rich dies disgraced.' He gave away his fortune before death --- and his name lives forever.)}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Nhung gi nguoi khac gieo vao doi ban --- du la mot co hoi nho --- tao ra mot nghi vu tham lang chuyen di luon trao xuong cho doi sau.\\
  \textit{(What others sow into your life --- even a small opportunity --- creates a silent obligation that flows on to the next generation.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Khong Tu Va Vu Khi Vo Hinh}
\begin{truyen}{Khong Tu Va Vu Khi Vo Hinh}{Khong Tu --- Trung Hoa TK 5 TCN}
\chuhoa{M}{}M ot hoc tro hoi Khong Tu: 'Thay co su that rang lam dieu tot se duoc bao dap va lam dieu ac se bi trung phat?'\\
\textit{(A student asked Confucius: 'Is it truly the case that doing good brings reward and doing evil brings punishment?')}

Khong Tu tra loi: 'It nhan bi at nhan phat hien ngay; nhung nhung van de sau nhieu nam sau moi hien ro.'\\
\textit{(Confucius replied: 'Immediate revenge is visible; but consequences often take years to become clear.')}

'Con nguoi khong phai la nguoi thi hanh, nhung su that chinh là nhan cach cua ho truoc canh nhan bao.'\\
\textit{('People are not the enforcers, but the truth is in their character --- as its own judge.')}

Ong ke chuyen mot nguoi trong boa tren duong di --- nhieu nam sau con chau ong duoc bong mat tren chinh con duong do.\\
\textit{(He told of a man who planted a tree along a road --- years later his descendants enjoyed shade on that very road.)}

'Nhan qua khong phai la phan thuong hay hinh phat --- no la su tiep noi tu nhien cua moi hanh dong lam on doi.'\\
\textit{('Karma is not reward or punishment --- it is the natural continuation of every action rippling through the world.')}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Hanh dong tot khong can chung nhan --- no tac dong nhu song tu tren mat nuoc, lan rong bang nhung cach ban khong nhin thay.\\
  \textit{(Good actions need no witnesses --- they ripple like waves on water, spreading in ways you cannot see.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Thien Si Nhat Ban Ci Matsutaro Shoriki}
\begin{truyen}{Thien Si Nhat Ban Ci Matsutaro Shoriki}{Matsutaro Shoriki --- Nhat Ban 1924}
\chuhoa{K}{}K hi Nhật Báo Yomiuri Shimbun sap pha san vao nam 1924, Matsutaro Shoriki mua lai to bao nay bang ca tai san cuoi cung cua minh.\\
\textit{(When the Yomiuri Shimbun was about to go bankrupt in 1924, Matsutaro Shoriki bought the paper with his last personal fortune.)}

Nhieu nguoi cho rang ong dien khi dau tu vao mot to bao tat nghiep; nhung ong tin rang bao chi can thiet cho xa hoi.\\
\textit{(Many thought him foolish for investing in a failing paper; but he believed journalism was necessary for society.)}

Ong giu to bao song --- nhung con lam hon: dua bong chay ve Nhat va phat trien cong nghiep truyen hinh.\\
\textit{(He kept the paper alive --- and did much more: he brought baseball to Japan and developed the television industry.)}

Khi nguoi ta hoi sao ong dam mao hiem, ong noi: 'Toi khong mao hiem --- toi dang gieo hat vao mot dat ma chinh toi cung se song tren do.'\\
\textit{(When asked why he dared to risk, he said: 'I wasn't taking a risk --- I was sowing seeds in a land I myself would also live in.')}

Yomiuri Shimbun tro thanh to bao phat hanh lon nhat the gioi voi hon 14 trieu ban moi ngay.\\
\textit{(Yomiuri Shimbun became the world's highest-circulation newspaper with over 14 million copies daily.)}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Khi ban gieo hat vao xa hoi that su ban dang song trong do --- ban khong dau tu, ban dang cham soc chinh ngoi nha cua ban.\\
  \textit{(When you sow seeds into the society you actually live in --- you're not investing, you're tending your own home.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Nguoi Tho Ren Va Ke Trom}
\begin{truyen}{Nguoi Tho Ren Va Ke Trom}{Truyen Co La Ma --- TK 2}
\chuhoa{M}{}M ot nguoi tho ren La Ma noi tieng voi su cong bang trong viec giao thuong --- khong bao gio can thi gian khi tre lai.\\
\textit{(A Roman blacksmith was famous for his fair dealings --- never shortchanging time or quality in return.)}

Mot ngay, mot ten trom vao xin viec va an cap mot con dao quy cua ong, roi bien mat.\\
\textit{(One day, a thief came asking for work and stole a valuable knife from him, then disappeared.)}

Muoi nam sau, ten trom ay --- bay gio da thanh nguoi luong thien --- tim ve tra lai con dao va tien chuoc loi.\\
\textit{(Ten years later, the thief --- now an honest man --- returned to give back the knife and money to make amends.)}

Nguoi tho ren hoi: 'Sao nguoi lai quay lai?' Ten trom tra loi: 'Vi suot muoi nam, con dao do nang trai tim toi.'\\
\textit{(The blacksmith asked: 'Why did you come back?' The thief replied: 'Because for ten years, that knife weighed on my heart.')}

Nguoi tho ren mim cuoi: 'Tinh luong thien trong nguoi khong det mat; no chi ngu yen doi khi.'\\
\textit{(The blacksmith smiled: 'The goodness in a person never dies; it only sleeps for a while.')}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Long luong thien trong moi con nguoi la mot hat giong --- co the bi chon vui nhung khong bao gio chet het; chi can dieu kien dung la no moc len.\\
  \textit{(The goodness in every person is a seed --- it may be buried but never fully dies; given the right conditions, it will always grow.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Martin Luther King Va Nhan Qua Bat Bao Luc}
\begin{truyen}{Martin Luther King Va Nhan Qua Bat Bao Luc}{Martin Luther King Jr. --- Hoa Ky 1963}
\chuhoa{K}{}K ing thuong xuyen bi de doa, bi danh bom nha, bi tu, bi dieu tra boi FBI --- nhung ong kien tri con duong bat bao luc.\\
\textit{(King was constantly threatened, had his home bombed, was jailed, monitored by the FBI --- but remained committed to nonviolence.)}

Ong tin rang neu phong trao dat quyen binh dang bang bao luc, ho se de lai mot xa hoi chua viet thuong day bao luc.\\
\textit{(He believed that if the movement achieved equality through violence, it would leave a society with fresh wounds full of new violence.)}

Ong noi: 'Bao luc tu vu thuong chi tao them bao luc --- ong si nhan va nguoc lai moi dut diem chuoi truyen.'' \\
\textit{(He said: 'Violence from violence only creates more violence --- patience and love alone can end the chain.')}

Cuoc hanh trinh tu Washington nam 1963 --- voi 250.000 nguoi dung yeu lang truoc Lincoln Memorial --- thay doi lich su that su.\\
\textit{(The March on Washington in 1963 --- with 250,000 standing quietly before the Lincoln Memorial --- truly changed history.)}

Nhan qua cua su kien nhan bat bao luc do: Dao Luat Dan Quyen 1964 va Dao Luat Quyen Bau Cu 1965.\\
\textit{(The karma of that patient nonviolence: the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965.)}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Hanh dong bat bao luc gieo nhan qua lau dai hon that bai vi no thay doi tam tri nguoi con lai, khong chi co the ho.\\
  \textit{(Nonviolent action sows longer-lasting karma than victory by force because it changes the minds of those who remain, not just their bodies.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Nguoi Dan Ba Trung Quoc Va Bao Gio Cung Tra On}
\begin{truyen}{Nguoi Dan Ba Trung Quoc Va Bao Gio Cung Tra On}{Truyen Co Trung Hoa --- TK 2}
\chuhoa{M}{}M ot nguoi dan ong ngheo trong lang lan dau mua ruong, nhung vu mua that bat --- ong sap dan vo tre don vao o.\\
\textit{(A poor village man had his first harvest fail badly --- he was about to take wife and children to live elsewhere.)}

Ba hang xom trong lang quyen gop thuc an va tien de giup gia dinh ong qua mua dong.\\
\textit{(Three neighbors in the village pooled food and money to help his family survive the winter.)}

Hai muoi nam sau, nguoi dan ong do tro nen giau co --- ong tim ve lan luot den nha ba nguoi hang xom cu.\\
\textit{(Twenty years later, the man became prosperous --- he traveled back and visited each of the three former neighbors.)}

Ong tra on ho --- khong phai vi luat le yeu cau, ma vi long biet on that su khong the dung yen trong long.\\
\textit{(He repaid them --- not because any law required it, but because genuine gratitude cannot sit still in the heart.)}

Mot trong so ho da mat; ong tim den con cai va tra on the he sau cua nguoi da giup ong.\\
\textit{(One of them had died; he found the children and repaid the next generation of the one who had helped him.)}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Long biet on that su khong doi thoi han --- no tim rac cach tra on du qua nhieu the he; do la nhan qua cua long nhan ai.\\
  \textit{(Genuine gratitude knows no statute of limitations --- it finds ways to repay even across generations; that is the karma of human kindness.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Hanh Dong Vo Tu Cua Nguoi Xay Gieng}
\begin{truyen}{Hanh Dong Vo Tu Cua Nguoi Xay Gieng}{Truyen Co Trung Dong --- Co dai}
\chuhoa{M}{}M ot nguoi xay gieng o giua sa mac chiu dong thanh tien rieng tuy, khong duoc ai y kien tra.\\
\textit{(A man built a well in the middle of a desert at his own expense, without anyone asking or offering reward.)}

Nguoi qua duong hoi: 'Ong co biet ten cua nhung nguoi se uong nuoc o day khong?' Ong luc dau.\\
\textit{(A passerby asked: 'Do you know the names of the people who will drink from this well?' He shook his head.)}

'Ong co biet ho se nho on ong khong?' Ong lai luc dau.\\
\textit{('Do you know if they will be grateful to you?' He shook his head again.)}

'Vay tai sao ong lam?' Ong tra loi: 'Vi ai do da xay gieng cho toi uong khi toi can --- va toi khong bao gio biet ten ho.'\\
\textit{('Then why do you do it?' He replied: 'Because someone built a well for me to drink when I needed it --- and I never knew their name.')}

'Toi chi lam tiep dieu da lam cho toi --- that vay la du'.\\
\textit{('I am only continuing what was done for me --- that is enough.')}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Vo tu la hinh thuc cao dep nhat cua nhan qua --- lam dieu tot vi no dung, khong phai vi ban mong doi duoc biet den.\\
  \textit{(Selflessness is the highest form of karma --- doing good because it is right, not because you hope to be known for it.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Nguoi Dau Bep Va Cach Tra On Cuoc Song}
\begin{truyen}{Nguoi Dau Bep Va Cach Tra On Cuoc Song}{Truyen Co Nhat Ban --- TK 12}
\chuhoa{M}{}M ot nguoi dau bep tre trong mot nha hang lon Nhat Ban noi tieng vi nau an tot lam.\\
\textit{(A young chef in a famous large Japanese restaurant was known for cooking unusually well.)}

Khi hoi ong bí mat la gi, ong tra loi mot cau bat ngo: 'Toi nau mon an nhu the toi dang tra on thuc pham.'\\
\textit{(Asked about his secret, he gave an unexpected answer: 'I cook as if I am thanking the ingredients.')}

'Con ga chet de nuoi toi --- toi co trach nhiem che bien no sao cho xung voi su hi sinh do.'\\
\textit{('The chicken died to nourish me --- I have a responsibility to prepare it in a way worthy of that sacrifice.')}

'Con ca, hat gao, cu hanh --- moi thu deu den tu dat va bien va mat moi nguoi lao dong.'\\
\textit{('The fish, the rice, the onion --- everything comes from the earth and sea and the labor of many hands.')}

Hanh dong nau an vo cung can than cua ong la mot hinh thuc tran trong nhan qua --- noi biet on qua tung mon an.\\
\textit{(His extraordinarily careful cooking was a form of honoring karma --- expressing gratitude through each dish.)}

\end{truyen}
\begin{baihoc}
  Bat ky viec gi ban lam voi long tran trong --- du la nau an, trong cay hay viet code --- deu la cach tra on cuoc song da trao cho ban.\\
  \textit{(Whatever you do with deep respect --- whether cooking, planting trees, or writing code --- is a way of giving thanks for the life given to you.)}
\end{baihoc}
