\section{Viec Nhe Lam Lau Thanh Mong Luc}
\begin{truyen}{Viec Nhe Lam Lau Thanh Mong Luc}{Doing Easy Work Too Long Softens the Will}
\chuhoa{C}{o nguoi tre rat thich nhung viec khong can va cham nhieu, khong can suy nghi sau, va cung it bi che.}
\textit{(Some young people prefer work that requires little friction, little deep thought, and little exposure to criticism.)}

Nhung viec do cho cam giac an toan va de tho hon truoc mat.
\textit{(That kind of work feels safe and comfortable in the short term.)}

Nhung neu ngay nao cung chi lam phan de, co bap nghe nghiep se yeu di ma minh khong nhan ra.
\textit{(But if every day contains only the easy part, professional muscles weaken without being noticed.)}

Cong viec qua em trong thoi gian dai co the lay mat suc chiu kho cua mot nguoi tre.
\textit{(Work that stays too comfortable for too long can quietly take away a young person’s endurance.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Su de chiu keo dai khong phai luc nao cung la dau hieu tot. Co khi no la dau hieu em dang dung yen.
\textit{(Long comfort is not always a good sign. Sometimes it means you are standing still.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Long comfort is not always a good sign.

Sometimes it means you are standing still.

\end{ghinhoanh}

\section{Tuong Cu Dung Vua Du La On}
\begin{truyen}{Tuong Cu Dung Vua Du La On}{Thinking Bare Minimum Effort Is Good Enough}
\chuhoa{C}{o nguoi tre rat gioi dung dung muc vua du: vua du de khong bi nhac, vua du de qua ngay, vua du de trong nhu van on.}
\textit{(Some young people become very skilled at doing just enough: enough to avoid being called out, enough to get through the day, enough to look fine.)}

Muc vua du do giu ho khoi rac roi trong ngan han.
\textit{(That minimum keeps them out of trouble in the short term.)}

Nhung no cung giu ho cach xa rat lau voi phien ban tot hon cua chinh minh.
\textit{(But it also keeps them far away from a stronger version of themselves for a very long time.)}

Lam du de song qua ngay khac hoan toan voi lam du tot de xay mot doi song dang song.
\textit{(Doing enough to survive the day is very different from doing enough well to build a worthwhile life.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Song vua du qua lau de lam nguoi ta quen voi mot phien ban thap hon kha nang that.
\textit{(Living at the bare minimum for too long makes people get used to a version below their real potential.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Living at the bare minimum for too long

makes you settle below your real potential.

\section{So Viec Kho Nen Cu De Danh Sau}
\begin{truyen}{So Viec Kho Nen Cu De Danh Sau}{Fearing Hard Work and Always Pushing It Later}
\chuhoa{N}{hieu nguoi biet ro viec nao moi la viec can xu ly som, nhung cu de no lai sau cung vi moi nghi den da thay nang.}
\textit{(Many people know exactly which task should be handled first, yet keep pushing it to the end because it already feels heavy in their mind.)}

Ho tuong de sau mot chut se de tho hon.
\textit{(They assume postponing it a bit will feel easier.)}

Nhung viec kho de sau thuong khong nhe di. No chi tich them ap luc, them so hai, va them cam giac minh dang tron.
\textit{(But hard work left for later rarely becomes lighter. It only gathers more pressure, fear, and the sense of avoidance.)}

Chon phan de truoc co the cho mot buoi nhe hon, nhung thuong de lai mot doi song nang hon.
\textit{(Choosing the easy part first may create an easier afternoon, but it often creates a heavier life.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Dieu kho nhat thuong khong nam o viec kho. No nam o thoi gian em dung de ne no.
\textit{(The hardest part is often not the task itself, but the time spent avoiding it.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

The hardest part is often not the task itself,

but the time spent avoiding it.

\section{O Lau Mot Cho Chi Vi Quen Tay}
\begin{truyen}{O Lau Mot Cho Chi Vi Quen Tay}{Staying Too Long Just Because It Feels Familiar}
\chuhoa{C}{o nguoi biet cong viec hien tai khong con day them gi dang ke, nhung van o lai vi moi thu da quen.}
\textit{(Some people know their current job no longer teaches them much, yet they stay because everything feels familiar.)}

Su quen thuoc cho cam giac do so hon nhung buoc chuyen moi.
\textit{(Familiarity feels less frightening than a new transition.)}

Nhung co nhung cho o lai them khong phai la ben, ma la tri hoan quyet dinh kho.
\textit{(But some forms of staying are not stability. They are simply delayed hard decisions.)}

Khi minh chi o lai vi da quen tay, minh dang tra tien bang thoi gian de doi lay cam giac it lo hon.
\textit{(When you stay only because it feels familiar, you pay with time in exchange for less anxiety.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Quen khong phai luc nao cung dung. Co khi no chi la noi so duoc lap lai du lau.
\textit{(Familiar does not always mean right. Sometimes it is just fear repeated for too long.)}
\end{baihoc}

\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Familiar does not always mean right.

\section{Muon Thu Nhap Tot Hon, Nhung Ngai Do Kho Tang}
\begin{truyen}{Muon Thu Nhap Tot Hon, Nhung Ngai Do Kho Tang}{Wanting Better Income While Avoiding Greater Difficulty}
\chuhoa{C}{o ban tre muon luong kha hon, vi tri tot hon, nhung lai muon cong viec van nhe nhu cu.}
\textit{(Some young people want better income and a stronger position, yet still hope the work stays just as easy.)}

\textit{(If you want the reward but resist the difficulty, you will resent life instead of rising to match it.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Muon buoc len mot tang moi, em phai chap nhan trong luong cua tang do.
\textit{(If you want to move to a higher level, you must accept the weight that comes with it.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Tuong Ban Ron La Dang Tien Len}

\chuhoa{N}{hieu nguoi luon co viec de lam, luon co lich de day, va luon thay minh khong ranh mot chut nao.}
\textit{(Many people are always active, always scheduled, and almost never free.)}

\begin{baihoc}
Tien len khong duoc do bang muc do tat bat, ma do bang gia tri duoc tich lai.
\textit{(Progress is not measured by how busy you are, but by the value you accumulate.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Progress is measured by the value you accumulate,

not by how busy you are.

\section{Chon Viec Vua Suc Toi Muc Khong Con Thu Thach}
\begin{truyen}{Chon Viec Vua Suc Toi Muc Khong Con Thu Thach}{Choosing Only Safe Work Until No Challenge Remains}
\chuhoa{C}{o nguoi rat it khi nhan viec minh chua chac, vi so lam khong tot se lo cai kem cua minh ra.}
\textit{(Some people rarely take tasks beyond their certainty because they fear exposing weakness.)}

\textit{(So they preserve comfort and are rarely forced into fast growth.)}

Nhung duong an toan qua muc thuong dan toi mot phien ban rat ben ngoai la on, ben trong la dang dung.
\textit{(But an overly safe path often creates a version of life that looks stable outside while standing still inside.)}

Thu thach khong de chiu, nhung thieu no thi nang luc rat kho day len mot tang moi.
\textit{(Challenge is uncomfortable, but without it ability rarely rises to a new level.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Neu luc nao em cung o trong vung chac chan, rat co the em dang doi su an toan lay toc do lon len.
\textit{(If you always stay inside certainty, you may be trading growth speed for safety.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

If you always stay inside certainty,

you may be trading growth for safety.

\section{Nghi It Nho, Tien It Xa}
\begin{truyen}{Nghi It Nho, Tien It Xa}{A Little Less Fear, A Little Further Ahead}
\chuhoa{S}{au nhieu lan mac ket trong vung de, co nguoi bat dau chu dong cham vao phan viec kho hon mot chut.}
\textit{(After many seasons stuck in the easy zone, some people begin reaching for slightly harder work on purpose.)}

Ho van so, van met, van bi goi y, nhung da khong con muon danh doi ca tuoi tre lay su yen on gia.
\textit{(They are still afraid, tired, and corrected, but no longer want to exchange youth for false calm.)}

Moi lan dam nhan mot chut nang hon, ho thay minh khong chi lam duoc viec, ma con lon them trong cach dung truoc viec kho.
\textit{(Each time they carry a little more weight, they see not only better work, but also a stronger self.)}

Luc do ho moi hieu dieu de nhat khong phai la viec kho. Dieu de nhat la dung qua lau trong mot doi song khong con day minh lon.
\textit{(Then they understand that the hardest thing is not difficulty itself, but staying too long in a life that no longer grows them.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Lon len hiem khi bat dau bang mot cu nhay rat xa. No thuong bat dau bang mot lan dung cham vao dieu minh van ne.
\textit{(Growth rarely begins with a giant leap. It usually begins with one touch on the thing you keep avoiding.)}
\end{baihoc}

\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Growth rarely begins with a giant leap.
