\section{Bi Gop Y La Thay Minh Bi Ha Thap}
\begin{truyen}{Bi Gop Y La Thay Minh Bi Ha Thap}{Hearing Feedback as Personal Diminishment}
\chuhoa{C}{o nguoi chi can bi nhac mot loi nho cung thay nhu minh bi danh gia ca con nguoi.}
\textit{(Some people hear one small correction and feel as though their whole character is being judged.)}

Vi tu ai noi len qua nhanh, ho phan ung truoc khi kip hieu dieu dang duoc gop y.
\textit{(Because ego rises too quickly, they react before understanding the feedback.)}

Phan ung do giu mat trong vai phut, nhung lam cham viec sua minh trong nhieu thang.
\textit{(That reaction may save face for a few minutes, but delays real improvement for months.)}

Mot cai toi qua nhay cam khien nguoi ta kho lon du rat thong minh.
\textit{(An overly sensitive ego makes growth difficult even for intelligent people.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Gop y dung khong lam em nho di. No chi cho em mot cho de lon them.
\textit{(Accurate feedback does not make you smaller. It simply reveals a place where you can grow.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Accurate feedback does not make you smaller.

It simply reveals a place where you can grow.

\end{ghinhoanh}

\section{Tranh Luan Chi De Khong Phai Thua}
\begin{truyen}{Tranh Luan Chi De Khong Phai Thua}{Arguing Only to Avoid Losing}
\chuhoa{C}{o nguoi vao mot cuoc noi chuyen khong de tim ra dieu dung hon, ma de giu minh o vi tri khong phai la nguoi sai.}
\textit{(Some people enter a conversation not to find what is more true, but to avoid being the one who looks wrong.)}

Vi the, moi ly le deu duoc huy dong de bao ve the dien.
\textit{(So every argument gets used to defend appearance.)}

Nhung mot cuoc tranh luan ma cai toi nam tay lai thuong khong de lai tri tue, chi de lai met moi va khoang cach.
\textit{(But an argument led by ego rarely leaves wisdom behind. It usually leaves exhaustion and distance.)}

Cang co hon thua, nguoi ta cang it hoc duoc dieu dang le phai hoc.
\textit{(The more people care about winning, the less they learn what they most need to learn.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Khong phai cuoc noi chuyen nao cung can co nguoi thang. Nhieu luc, dieu can nhat la co nguoi biet dung lai de hieu hon.
\textit{(Not every conversation needs a winner. Often what matters most is that someone stops long enough to understand better.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Not every conversation needs a winner.

Sometimes it only needs someone willing to understand better.

\end{ghinhoanh}

Nguoi hoi som co the ngai mot chut. Nguoi khong hoi thuong tra gia bang nhieu lan vo vao cung mot cho.

\begin{baihoc}

\section{Tu Trong That Khac Voi Giu Ve}
\begin{truyen}{Tu Trong That Khac Voi Giu Ve}{Real Self-Respect Is Different from Saving Face}
\chuhoa{S}{au nhieu va cham, co nguoi tre bat dau thay ro rang co nhung luc minh goi la tu trong, nhung that ra chi la khong muon bi cham vao cai toi.}
\textit{(After enough collisions, some young people begin to see that what they called self-respect was sometimes just an unwillingness to let the ego be touched.)}

Tu trong that giu cho mot nguoi khong de dang tu ha thap minh.
\textit{(Real self-respect keeps a person from cheaply lowering themselves.)}

Nhung no khong can ngan hoi, ngan sua, hay ngan xin loi khi can.
\textit{(But it does not prevent asking, correcting, or apologizing when necessary.)}

Khi phan biet duoc hai dieu do, mot nguoi bat dau song vung hon ma van mem du de hoc.
\textit{(Once the two are separated clearly, a person can live more firmly while still staying soft enough to learn.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Tu trong that khong dong cua hoc hoi. No chi giup em buoc vao hoc hoi ma khong danh roi pham gia.
\textit{(Real self-respect does not close the door to learning. It helps you enter learning without dropping your dignity.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Real self-respect does not close the door to learning.

It helps you learn without losing dignity.

\section{Sai Ro Rang Nhung Khong Muon Xin Loi}

Noi “em chua biet” dung luc la mot bieu hien cua tri tue sach.

\section{Thua Mot Lan La Khong Dam Quay Lai}
\begin{truyen}{Thua Mot Lan La Khong Dam Quay Lai}{Failing Once and Never Returning}
\chuhoa{C}{o nguoi thu mot lan, vap mot lan, que mot lan, roi tu hua se khong bao gio cham lai chuyen do nua.}
\textit{(Some people try once, fail once, feel embarrassed once, and then promise never to touch that thing again.)}

Ho goi do la biet than biet phan.
\textit{(They call it knowing their place.)}

Nhung nhieu khi do chi la si dien bi dau nen khong dam cho minh mot co hoi lam lai.

Mot lan que khong dinh nghia ca nang luc. No chi thu xem em co du khiem ton de quay lai hay khong.
\textit{(One embarrassing failure does not define all ability. It only tests whether you are humble enough to return.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Nguoi vuot len khong it lan xau ho hon nguoi khac. Ho chi khong lay xau ho lam diem dung.
\textit{(People who rise are not necessarily embarrassed less. They simply do not make embarrassment a stopping point.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

They do not make embarrassment

their stopping point.

\section{Cang Tu Ve Cang Cham Lon}
\begin{truyen}{Cang Tu Ve Cang Cham Lon}{The More Defensive You Are, the Slower You Grow}
\chuhoa{N}{hieu nguoi rat thong minh, nhung moi khi duoc nhac den cho can sua, ho lai vo thuc tu ve, giai thich, va lay ly do ra che.}
\textit{(Many intelligent people unconsciously defend, explain, and cover themselves whenever correction appears.)}

Moi lan tu ve nhu vay, ho giu duoc cai toi nhung bo qua mot co hoi cap nhat ban than.
\textit{(Each defensive reaction protects the ego but wastes a chance to update the self.)}

Nguoi hoc nhanh nhat thuong khong phai nguoi it sai nhat, ma la nguoi it mac ket nhat trong viec bao ve cai sai cua minh.
\textit{(The fastest learners are not always those who make the fewest mistakes, but those least trapped in defending their mistakes.)}

Tu ai qua day co the bien mot nguoi co to chat tot thanh mot nguoi lon rat cham.
\textit{(Dense pride can turn a promising person into a very slow-growing one.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Neu em muon lon nhanh, hay hoc cach nghe mot dieu kho nghe ma khong can lap tuc tu ve.
\textit{(If you want to grow quickly, learn to hear something hard without immediately defending yourself.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Learn to hear something hard

without immediately defending yourself.

\end{ghinhoanh}

\section{Ha Cai Toi Xuong De Di Xa Hon}
\begin{truyen}{Ha Cai Toi Xuong De Di Xa Hon}{Lowering the Ego to Go Further}
\chuhoa{S}{au nhieu lan vuot qua xau ho, co nguoi tre bat dau nhin gop y bang con mat khac.}
\textit{(After many awkward moments, some young people begin to look at feedback differently.)}

Ho khong con can luc nao cung phai dung ve phia cai toi cua minh.
\textit{(They no longer need to stand with their ego all the time.)}

Moi lan nhan sai som, hoi them som, va sua som, ho thay cuoc doi minh di nhe hon ma lai xa hon.
\textit{(By admitting wrong earlier, asking earlier, and correcting earlier, life becomes lighter and goes further.)}

Luc do ho moi thay tu trong that khong nam o viec khong bao gio sai. No nam o viec sai ma van giu duoc can dam de sua.
\textit{(Then they understand that real self-respect does not lie in never being wrong. It lies in having the courage to correct yourself after being wrong.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Ha cai toi xuong khong lam em nho di. No mo duong cho phien ban tot hon cua em di ra.
\textit{(Lowering the ego does not make you smaller. It opens the way for a better version of you to appear.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Lowering the ego does not make you smaller.

It opens the way for a better version of you to appear.

\end{ghinhoanh}
