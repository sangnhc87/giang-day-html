\section{Nhóm Chat Im Dần}
\begin{truyen}{Nhóm Chat Im Dần}{The Group Chat Slowly Goes Quiet}
\chuhoa{N}{hom ban than ngay xua tung noi chuyen suot ngay, nhung roi moi nguoi bat dau tra loi cham hon.}
\textit{(A close friend group once talked all day, but slowly everyone starts replying more slowly.)}

Co nguoi tuong su im lang do co nghia tinh ban dang chet.
\textit{(Someone assumes that silence means friendship is dying.)}

Nhung lon len la luc lich song tan ra, trach nhiem day vao, va su than thiet khong con do bang tan suat tin nhan nua.
\textit{(But growing up spreads schedules apart, pushes in responsibilities, and stops measuring closeness by message count alone.)}

Dieu can hoc la nhin tinh ban bang do that, khong chi bang do on ao.
\textit{(The lesson is to measure friendship by truth, not only by noise.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Tinh ban truong thanh co luc im hon, nhung khong he nhe hon.
\textit{(Mature friendship may become quieter, but not lighter.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

Mature friendship may become quieter, but not lighter.

\end{ghinhoanh}

\section{Bạn Thành Công Trước}

\textit{(But the sting is deeply human.)}

Van de khong nam o cam xuc dau, ma o cach em xu ly no sach hay doc.
\textit{(The real issue is whether you process it cleanly or turn it bitter.)}

Neu giu duoc su trong sang truoc thanh cong cua ban, em lon len rat nhieu.
\textit{(If you stay clean-hearted before a friend’s success, you grow a lot.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Tinh ban ben can long mung cho nhau, nhung cung can su thanh that voi vet cham cua chinh minh.
\textit{(Stable friendship needs joy for each other, but also honesty about your own sting.)}
\end{baihoc}

\section{Giữ Vì Kỷ Niệm}
\begin{truyen}{Giữ Vì Kỷ Niệm}{Holding On Only Because of Memory}
\chuhoa{C}{o nguoi tiep tuc mot tinh ban da met moi chi vi so bo di se phu long qua khu.}
\textit{(Someone keeps a tiring friendship alive only because leaving feels like betraying the past.)}

Ho tuong ky niem dep la ly do du de keo mot moi quan he di tiep.
\textit{(They think good memories alone are enough reason to drag a relationship forward.)}

Nhung ky niem nen duoc cam on, khong nen bi ep thanh day xich cho hien tai.
\textit{(But memories should be thanked, not forced into chains that bind the present.)}

\textit{(Ending an unfitting bond does not erase its beautiful memories.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Ky niem la dieu de nho, khong phai ly do de tu lam kho minh mai.
\textit{(Memory is something to honor, not a reason to hurt yourself forever.)}

\section{Quý Người Còn Ở Lại}
\begin{truyen}{Quý Người Còn Ở Lại}{Valuing the Friends Who Still Remain}
\chuhoa{S}{au nhieu lan tan ra, co nguoi bat dau thay ro ai van nho, van hoi, van xuat hien khi can.}
\textit{(After many drifting seasons, someone finally sees clearly who still remembers, asks, and shows up when needed.)}

Truoc do ho tuong phai giu tat ca moi la song co tinh.
\textit{(Before that, they thought keeping everyone was the most heartfelt way to live.)}

\textit{(But sometimes the wealth of friendship is not in the number of contacts, but in the few who truly remain.)}

Biet quy mot vai nguoi ben lanh manh hon viec co gang om het nhung quan he da rai het.
\textit{(Valuing a few healthy bonds is wiser than holding all frayed ones.)}

\end{truyen}

\begin{baihoc}
Ban than khong can dong. Chi can that.
\textit{(True friends do not need to be many. They need to be real.)}
\end{baihoc}

\begin{ghinhoanh}
\textbf{Short English to remember:}

True friends do not need to be many.
